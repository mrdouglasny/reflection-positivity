## The two proof routes (choose one ‚Äî see ¬ßVet)

**Route A ‚Äî Gaussian crossing perfect square (classical GJ 6.2.2).**
1. **Action split (easy).** `V` is single-site, `r` permutes sites ‚üπ `Œ£ V(œÜ_x)` is Œ∏-invariant;
   the kinetic bonds split into (within-Œõ‚Çä) + (within-Œõ‚Çã = reflected Œõ‚Çä) + **crossing bonds**
   (each joins `x‚àà‚àÇŒõ‚Çä` to `r x‚àà‚àÇŒõ‚Çã`). [pphi2 `action_decomposition` is the V-side, generalizes.]
2. **Half integration.** For `mPos`-measurable `F`, `‚à´ F¬∑(F‚àòŒ∏) dŒº` factors over the plane config
   into `‚à´_{plane} |G(œÜ‚Çä,plane)|¬≤-shaped ¬∑ (crossing kernel)` ‚Äî the within-halves + V pieces give
   the modulus-square (identical `Œõ‚Çä`/`Œõ‚Çã` structure via Œ∏-symmetry), leaving the crossing kernel.
3. **HARD CORE ‚Äî the crossing perfect square.** The crossing term
   `exp(‚àí¬Ω Œ£_{x‚àà‚àÇŒõ‚Çä} J_x (œÜ_x ‚àí œÜ_{r x})¬≤) = exp(‚àí¬Ω J_x œÜ_x¬≤)¬∑exp(‚àí¬Ω J_x œÜ_{rx}¬≤)¬∑exp(Œ£ J_x œÜ_x œÜ_{rx})`.
   The quadratic self-terms absorb into the halves; the **cross term** `exp(Œ£ J_x œÜ_x œÜ_{rx})`
   with `J_x ‚â• 0` is a **positive-definite kernel** in `(œÜ_‚àÇ, œÜ_{r‚àÇ})`, so the whole integral is a
   perfect square `‚ü®g, g‚ü© ‚â• 0`. This is the generic form of pphi2's current N-specific axiom
   `gaussian_rp_cov_perfect_square` (`OSProofs/OS3_RP_Lattice.lean`, still an axiom on the square
   side too). ‚öÝ **Provable-or-axiom decision (the crux):** `exp(J œÜ œà)`, `J ‚â• 0`, is PD by
   Hubbard‚ÄìStratonovich: `exp(J œÜ œà) = ùîº_{Œª~N(0,J)}[exp(ŒªœÜ)exp(Œªœà)]` (or the finite-dim
   `exp(¬Ω‚ü®v,Av‚ü©)` with `A` the crossing coupling PSD via `J_x ‚â• 0`). Mathlib has Gaussian-integral
   machinery; whether this closes cleanly or stays a **vetted axiom** (as on the square side) is
   the main open question. If it stays an axiom, the WIN is still large: ONE generic PD/perfect-
   square axiom replaces per-geometry ones, and everything else (split, integration, assembly) is
   generic and proved.

**Route B ‚Äî Osterwalder‚ÄìSeiler transfer positivity.**
RP ‚ü∫ the transfer operator `T` across the reflection plane is positive self-adjoint, via
Feynman‚ÄìKac `‚à´ F¬∑(F‚àòŒ∏) dŒº = ‚ü®T^{1/2}Œ¶_F, T^{1/2}Œ¶_F‚ü© ‚â• 0`. pphi2 already builds the asym transfer
system (`asymTransferSystem`), proved the transfer operator **positive kernel + self-adjoint +
compact** (`AsymL2Operator`, `AsymJentzsch`), and has the trace dictionary
(`TransferSystem.twoPoint_dictionary`, `GroundBridge`). Route B would reuse this heavily. Cost:
connecting the **full RP form over arbitrary positive-time `F`** (not just two-point functions)
to the transfer operator needs a Feynman‚ÄìKac representation of `‚à´ F¬∑(F‚àòŒ∏) dŒº` as an operator
inner product ‚Äî the trace dictionary currently covers two-point observables, so this needs a
generalization (`F` = product of slice observables ‚üπ `Œ¶_F` = the corresponding vector). Route B
avoids Hubbard‚ÄìStratonovich entirely; its hard core is instead the FK representation lemma.
